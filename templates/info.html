About Computer Vision Syndrome (CVS)

What is CVS? — Computer Vision Syndrome (also called digital eye strain) refers to a group of eye and vision-related problems resulting from prolonged digital device use.

Common symptoms
  • Eye strain / tired eyes
  • Dry or irritated eyes
  • Blurred or double vision
  • Headaches or neck/shoulder pain
Common causes
  • Prolonged screen time without breaks
  • Poor lighting or glare on the screen
  • Improper viewing distances & posture
  • Uncorrected vision problems
Remedies & preventive steps
  • Follow the 20–20–20 rule: every 20 minutes look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds
  • Adjust screen brightness and contrast; use proper ambient lighting
  • Maintain correct posture and screen distance (about arm's length)
  • Use artificial tears for dryness if needed and consult an eye care professional
